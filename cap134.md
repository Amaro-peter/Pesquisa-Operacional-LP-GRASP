## 4. Failure Case Analysis & LP Fractionality Discussion

### The Effect of LP Relaxation Fractionality
The LP-Biased Hybrid GRASP solver relies on the fractional facility opening values $y_f \in [0, 1]$ from the Simplex solution as sampling probabilities. 

1. **Naturally Integral / Easy Cases (e.g. `cap134.txt`):**
   In Euclidean UFLP instances like Beasley's OR-Library problems, the LP relaxation is often naturally integral (LP duality gap is extremely close to 0%, and almost all facilities have $y_f \in \{0, 1\}$). 
   In these cases, the LP-biased probabilities are either 1 or 0 (with a small epsilon floor of 0.01).    This means the constructive phase samples the exact globally optimal facilities, generating starting solutions    with 0% optimality gaps. Consequently, the local search phase converges in 0 iterations because the initial solution    is already a global optimum.

2. **Fractional / Hard Cases (Generated Suite):**
   As we increase the setup costs and add non-Euclidean noise (which violates the triangle inequality), the LP relaxation    becomes highly fractional. The LP solver opens many facilities partially (e.g. $y_f = 0.2$) to distribute service    assignments fractionally. This causes the LP duality gap to widen to over 15%.
   - **Sampling Degradation:** Because the probabilities $y_f$ are fractional and spread across many facilities, the random      constructive sampling cannot rely on clear binary signals. The sampler often selects sub-optimal combinations of      facilities, resulting in a substantial increase in the constructive phase's initial optimality gap (climbing from 0% up to 10-15%).
   - **Local Search Repair Effort:** When the initial solution degrades, the local search phase must work significantly harder      to repair the solution. We see a direct correlation: as the LP duality gap and the number of fractional facilities increase,      the number of local search iterations required to reach a local optimum increases dramatically (from 0 up to 10+ iterations).
   - **Hybrid Advantage vs. α-GRASP Baseline:** Despite the sampling degradation, the LP-biased constructive phase still      retains a clear spatial guide from the fractional values, yielding an initial optimality gap that is consistently lower      than the blind greedy $\alpha$-GRASP construction (which frequently starts with a gap of 15% to 25%). Furthermore,      the LP-biased hybrid method consistently converges to higher-quality local optima (frequently finding the exact optimal IP solution)      with fewer local search iterations than the baseline.
